COMMON CLOUD TYPES OVER WICHITA

Spot these clouds to help forecast incoming weather in Tornado Alley.

Cumulus
Puffy white fair-weather clouds
Can grow tall and turn into thunderstorms on hot days.
Cumulonimbus
Thunderstorm clouds
Towering anvil-shaped clouds that bring heavy rain, hail, lightning, and tornadoes.
Cirrus
High, thin wispy clouds
"Mare's tails" – often signal a weather change coming within 24 hours.
Stratus
Low, flat gray layer
Overcast sky that can bring light drizzle or fog.
Stratocumulus
Lumpy low clouds
Looks like a bumpy blanket – common in cooler weather.
Altocumulus
Mid-level "mackerel sky"
Rows of small puffs – can precede thunderstorms.

Cloud watching is a great skill for storm chasers in Kansas!
