COMMON CLOUD TYPES

Learn to spot these clouds while watching the sky over Wichita. Great for forecasting incoming weather!

Cumulus
Fluffy white "cotton ball" clouds • Fair weather
Puffy, vertical development. Can grow into thunderstorms if they become tall.
Cumulonimbus
Thunderstorm clouds • Heavy rain, hail, lightning
Towering anvil-shaped clouds. The classic severe weather producer in Kansas.
Cirrus
High, thin, wispy "mare's tails"
Often signal a change in weather approaching within 24 hours.
Stratus
Low, flat gray blanket • Drizzle possible
Overcast sky that can produce light steady rain or fog.
Stratocumulus
Lumpy low clouds • Light rain possible
Looks like a lumpy blanket. Common in cooler weather.
Altocumulus
Mid-level "mackerel sky" patches
Often appear as rows of small puffs. Can precede thunderstorms.

Cloud watching helps predict incoming weather in Tornado Alley. Cumulonimbus = time to watch radar closely!
