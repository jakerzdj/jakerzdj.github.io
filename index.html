COMMON CLOUD TYPES

Cumulus
Puffy white "cotton ball" clouds
Fair weather clouds. Can grow tall and become thunderstorms on hot, humid days.
Cumulonimbus
Towering thunderstorm clouds
Anvil-shaped. Bring heavy rain, hail, lightning, strong winds, and tornadoes.
Cirrus
High, thin wispy clouds
"Mare's tails" — often mean a weather change is coming within 24 hours.
Stratus
Low, flat gray blanket
Overcast sky that can produce light drizzle or fog.
Stratocumulus
Lumpy low clouds
Looks like a bumpy blanket. Common in cooler weather.
Altocumulus
Mid-level "mackerel sky"
Small puffs in rows — can precede thunderstorms.
